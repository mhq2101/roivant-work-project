Darapladib

  • Diabetic Macular Edema (DME) is an accumulation of fluid in the macula—part of the retina that controls our most detailed vision abilities—due to leaking blood vessels.
  • With the rapid increase of the Diabetic population, DME affects over 1.5 million people and current treatments are insufficient.
  • The current treatments can be dangerous and invasive, and mainly treat the symptoms, however, Darapladib is a non-invasive drug that treats DME at the source

Darapladib

Safe and Non-invasive
Once Daily Oral Supplement
Met Safety Goals in trial involving 14000 patients
Source Treatment
Inhibits enzyme Lp-PLA2 that causes the retinal capillaries to become permeable leading to edema.
Lp-PLA2 is upstream of VEGF in DME (Diabetic Macular Edema) pathology, thus treating source, rather than symptom, of inflammation in DME

Darapladib Clinical Trial

  • 12-week placebo-controlled, randomized, multi-center clinical trial (Phase 2)
  • Once-daily, oral darapladib improves visual acuity score by 5.2 letters in subgroup (baseline vision < 20/40) excluding patients with high baseline visual acuity
  • Phase-3 Lucentis trial enrolled patients with baseline vision < 20/40
